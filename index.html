Qualitative indicators (6) — what the experience shows

Member experience · Q1

Member voice

Peer Learning & Ownership · Domain G

Community shift in progress

Members in the Financial Resilience series requested a follow-up peer discussion space between sessions, indicating readiness to move from passive participation to active exchange. Two members offered to share their own reserve-building experience in session three. This is the shift from a platform for members to a community with members.

Decision question: Are members teaching one another? (Domain G – Peer Learning and Member Contribution)

Onboarding Effectiveness · Domain J

General member onboarding themes

First cohort flagged two consistent themes: (1) unclear expectations about what membership involves beyond attending sessions, and (2) limited sense of how their experience differs from active partners. Onboarding sequence is being revised to address both. Early signal: General Members who received a personal welcome call engaged at a higher rate in their first 30 days.

Decision question: Are onboarding pathways effective? (Domain J – Onboarding Effectiveness)

Accessibility & Inclusion · Domain I

Connectivity & inclusion signals

Connectivity issues reported by 12% of Q1 respondents, concentrated among 3 CSOs in rural border of Northeastern. Asynchronous access to session recordings mitigated some impact. Members in those geographies have been offered early access to session materials. Language and time zone considerations are being factored into Q2 scheduling.

Decision question: Who is not participating, and are some groups benefiting more than others? (Domain I – Accessibility and Inclusion)

Organisational Strengthening · Domain L

Early practice change signals

Three active partners reported that Q1 content directly informed an internal process or decision: one is developing an operating reserve policy; one revised its M&E reporting template using frameworks shared in pre-session materials; one cited TLC content in a funding proposal submitted in March 2026. These are early signals of TLC contributing to organisational practice, not just knowledge.

Decision question: Is TLC supporting Blood:Water's organisational strengthening goals? (Domain L)

Community Health & Culture · Domain K

Positive culture signals

Facilitator and platform manager observations across Q1 sessions indicate high levels of candour in discussion, including members sharing resource constraints and programme failures. No moderation incidents recorded. Members are engaging with each other, not only with the facilitator. This is a positive early signal that the community-of-practice culture is taking root.

Decision question: Do members feel safe contributing, and are Community Guidelines being lived out? (Domain K – Community Health and Culture)
