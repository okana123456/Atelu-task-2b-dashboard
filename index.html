What the experience shows

Member Experience · Q1

Member voice

Peer Learning & Ownership · Domain G

Community shift in progress

Members in the Financial Resilience series requested a follow-up peer discussion space between sessions, indicating readiness to move from passive participation to active exchange. Two members offered to share their own reserve-building experience in session three. This is the shift from a platform for members to a community with members.

Decision question: Are members teaching one another? (Domain G)

Onboarding Effectiveness · Domain J

General member onboarding themes

First cohort flagged two consistent themes: unclear expectations about what membership involves beyond attending sessions, and limited sense of how their experience differs from active partners. Early signal: General Members who received a personal welcome call engaged at a higher rate in their first 30 days.

Decision question: Are onboarding pathways effective? (Domain J)

Accessibility & Inclusion · Domain I

Connectivity & inclusion signals

Connectivity issues reported by 12% of Q1 respondents, concentrated in three countries. Asynchronous access to session recordings mitigated some impact. Language and time zone considerations are being factored into Q2 scheduling.

Decision question: Who is not participating? (Domain I)

Organisational Strengthening · Domain L

Early practice change signals

Three active partners reported that Q1 content directly informed an internal process or decision: one is developing an operating reserve policy; one revised its M&E reporting template; one cited TLC content in a funding proposal submitted in March 2026. These are early signals of TLC contributing to organisational practice, not just knowledge.

Decision question: Is TLC supporting Blood:Water's organisational strengthening goals? (Domain L)

Community Health & Culture · Domain K

Positive culture signals

Facilitator and platform manager observations across Q1 sessions indicate high levels of candour in discussion, including members sharing resource constraints and programme failures. No moderation incidents recorded. Members are engaging with each other, not only with the facilitator. This is a positive early signal that the community-of-practice culture is taking root.

Decision question: Do members feel safe contributing? (Domain K)

Beacon of Hope Finance team adopts scenario-planning tool

Following the Financial Resilience series, the finance team at Beacon of Hope adopted a shared scenario-planning tool with their board — the first step toward a formal operating reserve policy. This is a direct outcome linking TLC content to organisational decision-making in practice. (Linked to Q7 and Q11 member feedback.)
